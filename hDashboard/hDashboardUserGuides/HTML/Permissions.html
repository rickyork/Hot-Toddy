Access to most things on the website can be controlled and limited to specific people and/or specific groups of people, the process of controlling access to something is done by assigning permissions.

Permissions can be applied to a variety of items. You can limit access to a file, for example. But the concept of permissions does not stop at just files, it can also be applied to the folders that files reside within and things that don’t have anything to do with files or folders at all, such as contacts in an address book, address books, calendars, forums, forum topics, and a variety of other things.

Since permissions can be applied to a variety of things, the website abstracts terminology by referring to all items that can have permissions applied to them as resources.

Overall access to a a resource, can be controlled in the following ways:

  1. Whether or not the resource is available publicly (without a login). This type of access is called World access, because it means that you are controlling the right of access of any person in the world with an internet connection.

    Items with World access can be accessed or downloaded without a login, and can potentially be automatically indexed by search engines like Google, Bing, Yahoo, etc.

  2. Whether or not the resource’s creator can access a resource. This type of access is called Owner access, since it refers to the person who created the resource.

  3. Whether or not the resource can be accessed by individual users, a type of access called User access. Any individual user can be granted access to any resource.

  4. Whether or not the resource can be accessed by a group of users, and this of course is called Group access. Groups can be made up of one or more individual people or may also contain other groups.

Within the preceding types of access, World, Owner, User, and Group, you can further control the type of access granted by selecting one of two options:

Read Access - Allow the person(s) or group(s) access to look at or download a resource, but not the ability to change it. i.e., someone granted only Read Access does not have the ability to modify or delete the resource.

Write Access - Allow the person(s) or group(s) the ability to modify or delete a resource.

Managing Permissions

For the most part, you do not have to worry about manually assigning permissions to a resource.

Where it concerns files, folders, and categories:

  1. Files automatically inherit the permissions of the folder they are uploaded to.
  2. Folders automatically inherit the permissions of the folder the folder is created within.
  3. Categories automatically inherit the permissions of the category that category is created within.

The group “Website Administrators” is automatically granted Read and Write Access, then the department group is also automatically granted Read and Write access.

DON’T:

Don’t change or modify permissions for “Website Administrators”. Changing the permissions of this group will leave not only yourself, but everyone designated as a website administrator without the ability to access or modify the resource.

Mistakes can be fixed, but please avoid changing permissions associated with either of these groups.

DO:

Feel free to add any individual user or group.

Distributing a Document to an Outside Person or Group

Documents on the website can be easily and securely made available to people outside of your organization.

There are two ways to make a document available for outside access:

  1. Give the document Read Access under World. This makes a document publicly available without a login. DO NOT use this option if the document is sensitive in nature, as using this option makes the document available to anyone in the world.

Using this method, you can transmit a web URL to the document easily, by right-clicking on a file and clicking “Email Link”:

The “Email Link” option starts up your mail client with a link to the document included in the body of the message.

If the document has World with Read Access applied, the person on the receiving end will be able to simply click the link and open the document. If the document does not have World with Read Access, the person on the receiving end will be prompted to login to the website. Then, after a successful login, if that person has the right credentials, that person will be able to access the document. If not, the person will see a “Not Authorized” message and will be blocked from accessing the document.

  1. Another, more secure way, of granting access to a document to an outside resource can be done by assigning a password to the document.

You can assign a password to a document by right-clicking on the document and clicking “Passwords”.

NOTE: Transmitting a password by email is NOT secure. Email is more often than not transmitted across the internet unencrypted, meaning that a person with the right knowledge and skill could intercept your email message and obtain those passwords. If the document is sensitive in nature, you should remove the password from the email message and provide the password to the receiving party by phone or some other secured method.
