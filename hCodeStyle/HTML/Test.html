Disciplinary Counseling/Performance Improvement

  • Performance Improvement Plan Checklist
  • Performance Improvement Plan
  • Employee Response Form

Performance and behavior issues should be addressed as soon as they arise.

It is important to be consistent in the way that disciplinary counseling is handled for all employees in the department or office.

A Human Resources Consultant can be contacted for assistance regarding an employee’s performance or behavior.

Discussions with employees should be done privately, face to face and must be documented.

A Performance Improvement Plan form should be used to document employee performance problems.

  • The form should be used as a tool to improve performance as well as document the problem.
  • It is recommended that a completed Performance Improvement Plan be reviewed by a Human Resources Consultant before it is issued to the employee.
  • Time frames or dates should not be used as this may be considered a guarantee or promise of employment.
  • Employees should have the opportunity to provide a written response, but the manager should always retain the original Performance Improvement Plan. If employees want to make a response outside of the meeting, they should do so on a separate piece of paper or on their copy of the form. The response should be attached to the Performance Improvement Plan.
  • Employees should sign the Performance Improvement Plan. If they do not sign the form, a witness (preferably a member of management) should sign the form signifying that the Performance Improvement Plan was issued to the employee and he/she declined to sign the form.
  • Employees should receive a copy of the completed form and the original should be maintained in their personnel file.

A Documentation Log can be used for a manager to keep records of discussions or issues with employees when a formal Performance Improvement Plan is not issued.

Harassment Prohibition

Any complaints or reports of harassment should be reported immediately to a Human Resources Consultant or the Human Resources Manager.

Harassment is a form of inappropriate conduct that undermines the employment relationship. All employees (including physicians and management) are responsible for adhering to the Harassment Prohibition policy.

If an employee discusses an issue, but states that he/she does not want to do anything about it, managers are obligated to report it immediately to the Human Resources Department. Failing to report the incident to the Human Resources Department places the Company, the office and management at risk.

The Human Resources Department conducts thorough and impartial investigations of all complaints as timely and confidential a manner as possible.

Management may be asked for assistance in the investigation. Regardless of the involvement, all information must be kept confidential and should only be discussed with the Human Resources Department, unless otherwise directed.

It is illegal and against Company policy to retaliate against any person who makes a complaint in good faith.

Employees should contact the Human Resources Department if they have any questions or complaints regarding harassment.

Job & Pay Changes

  • Job & Pay Change

Job and Pay Changes should be made in UltiPro Manager Self Service.

The effective date should always be the first day of a pay period. The standard work week starts on Saturday and ends on Friday. Therefore, the effective date should be a Saturday. Refer to the Payroll Processing Calendar for the exact date.

Complete all sections that need to be changed. For example, if an employee is receiving a merit increase, the effective date should be entered, a reason selected, and the new rate entered. If an employee is changing from temporary to full-time status, the effective date should be entered, a reason selected, change the employee from Temp to Full-time, amend the hours to the bi-weekly amount, and change from Part-time to Full-time.

One entry can be used for multiple changes for an employee, if the changes all have the same effective date.

All entries must be submitted by the deadline for approval before being approved for payroll.

Family and Medical Leave Act

The Human Resources Department will process leaves under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA). It is the manager’s responsibility to notify a Human Resources Consultant that an employee may be eligible for or may need FMLA leave.

Employees may be eligible for leave under the FMLA after 12 months of service with American Health Network if they have worked at least 1250 hours in the previous 12 months.

Eligible employees are allowed 12 weeks of leave in a rolling 12 month period under the FMLA, which provides job and benefit protection for the employee.

FMLA leave is available to eligible employees for:

  • the birth, adoption or placement of a foster child with/of the employee; and care for the child;
  • the employee’s own serious health condition that makes them unable to work;
  • the care for a child, spouse or parent who has a serious health condition.

Managers should contact a Human Resources Consultant if an employee*:

  • has/is scheduled for a surgery or treatment that will cause an absence from work
  • has missed 2-3 days of work for his/her own illness or for an illness of a family member
  • misses work often because of his/her own illness or the illness of a family member
  • is pregnant
  • is in the process of adoption or providing foster care

* This is not a complete list; if unsure of a situation, contact a Human Resources Consultant.

Employees do not need to specifically state that they would like Family or Medical leave. It is up to management to offer it if they may be eligible.

All completed FMLA forms must be forwarded to a Human Resources Consultant immediately. By law, responses to requests must be made within two business days.

A Human Resources Consultant will advise the employee and manager whether or not the request for leave has been approved.

If an employee is approved for intermittent leave (leave on a reduced schedule or intermittent basis), mangers should track the employee’s days missed that are covered by the FMLA leave. An Attendance Record form may be used for tracking.

Employees must use any accrued PTO when using FMLA leave, unless covered by Short-Term Disability benefits.

Military Leave

American Health Network will comply with all applicable laws regarding military leaves of absence. Employees are requested to notify management as soon as they are aware of any military obligation.

Regular employees may be eligible for pay differential for up to 14 days annually for required training. Pay may be based on the difference between military and Company pay and employees must provide pay stub(s) from the government before their pay differential may be processed.

Military reservist employees and those volunteering for or called to active military duty are entitled to reemployment with the Company upon their return from duty in full compliance with all applicable federal and state laws. In addition, besides the military leave benefits discussed above, employees who request a military leave of absence may elect to use any accrued vacation pay in lieu of unpaid leave, and may elect to continue health care benefits to the extent permitted by law, during their leave of absence.

Short-Term Disability

Employees with a non-occupational illness or injury may be eligible for Short-term Disability (STD) benefits after 12 months of full-time service with American Health Network.

When an employee may be eligible for STD, he/she should be given an STD Claim Packet. The employee must complete the STD PTO Election Form, Employee Statement, and his/her physician must complete the Attending Physician’s Statement. Completed forms should be sent to a Human Resources Consultant, who will complete the Employer’s Report of Claim.

A Human Resources Consultant will then process the initial claim with the STD carrier. The manager will be informed when the claim has been approved or denied. STD benefits will not be paid until the claim is processed and approved by the STD carrier. Benefits will be processed through payroll for the next pay period.

Leave under STD will also count towards the twelve week allotment of Family or Medical Leave.

Employees must present a release to work statement from their physician before or upon returning to work. It is the manager’s responsibility to make sure that this documentation is received. The manager should notify a Human Resources Consultant of the exact day the employee returns to work and forward a copy of the release to work statement.

Any questions regarding Short-Term Disability should be directed to a Human Resources Consultant.

Performance Evaluation

Managers are encouraged to formally evaluate each employee’s performance at least annually. Performance reviews may also be done more frequently or on an informal basis during the year.

Depending on the status of the employee, managers should use the performance evaluation form for either Director, Manager, Supervisor or Employee.

Employees should receive a copy of the completed evaluation and the original should be maintained in their personnel file.

Performance and behavior should be documented in an objective manner and should include both positive and negative feedback.

Evaluations should be viewed as a performance improvement tool for the employee and as a way to document the employee’s performance and goals.

Any criteria on the evaluation form that is rated “Inconsistent work” or “Unacceptable work” should be explained and specific examples cited if needed or appropriate.

A Human Resources Consultant can assist with the evaluation process.

Refer to the Performance Evaluation Guidelines regarding writing and administering evaluations.

Personnel Files

  • HR File Record Retention

It is the responsibility of the office to have and maintain a personnel file for each employee. The Human Resources Department does not keep personnel files for employees in the offices.

All information in regards to employment should be kept in the employee’s personnel file.

  • Required documents: Employment Application, Signed acknowledgement for Employee Handbook, Code of Conduct, and HIPAA
  • Additional documents: New Hire/Rehire paperwork, Job and Pay Changes, Performance Evaluations, etc.

The files should be secured/locked and not accessible to the employees. Only those on a need-to-know basis should have access to employee information.

Once a performance related document has been filed, it should remain filed. It is not necessary to release the document to the employee at a later time.

All medical information (OSHA, Worker’s Compensation, TB testing, STD/FMLA) should be kept separately from the personnel file.

If the employee happens to be a patient, the patient file should be a separate file.

Personnel files must be kept for 5 years after termination.

All employment verification requests should be forwarded to the Human Resources Department.

Employees wanting to review their personnel file should make an appointment with management. Files should be reviewed while management is present. Copies of the file’s contents should not be made for employees.

Terminations

Contact a Human Resources Consultant before any involuntary termination.

At-Will Employment

  • All employment is at-will. The employee or the Company may at any time terminate the employment relationship with or without cause, reason or notice consistent with applicable state or federal law.
  • There is no probationary period.

An employee will not receive severance or pay in lieu of notice without a signed Release and Waiver. Management has the discretion to determine severance for Job Elimination, Lack of Work or Mutual Agreement terminations or pay in lieu of notice. Contact a Human Resources Consultant for a Release and Waiver for any money to be paid to an employee past their last day worked.

Employees should be given a Termination Information sheet, which provides them with information regarding their pay and benefits upon termination.

Voluntary Resignations

Employees should provide a written resignation to management.

Employees may contact HR for an exit interview.

Termination in Manager Self Service

Select the reason that most closely matches the reason for termination. Only one reason should be selected.

The “Effective Date” should be one of the following:

  • the last day worked*
  • the third day the employee missed work (unreported) for Job Abandonment **
  • the date the employee was scheduled to return to work from leave, but failed to return to work
  • the date the employee was notified of the termination

* Do not use the day after the last day worked. If unsure about the effective date, contact a Human Resources Consultant.

** For Job Abandonment, the Human Resources Department should be notified on or before the third day and a written confirmation should be sent to the employee regarding the termination.

The Payroll Department will calculate PTO payout. Be sure that all PTO requests have been submitted to Payroll before the date of termination. No PTO should be used/approved after a resignation has been submitted.

The Termination should be entered in Manager Self Service as soon as possible after the termination.

Workers Compensation

Workers Compensation Video

A Workers’ Compensation claim form (Indiana or Ohio) must be completed immediately upon knowledge of a work related injury or illness.

To clarify whether an illness or injury qualifies as a Workers’ Compensation claim, contact a Human Resources Consultant.

It is important that a Workers’ Compensation claim is filed immediately after the incident occurs. Delayed reporting can cause additional expenses for the employee, the office, and the Company.

The claim form should be completed as thoroughly as possible and faxed to the Human Resources Department at (317) 580-6343. A Human Resources Consultant will send the claim to our carrier for review and processing.

If the employee does not miss any work time and/or receives treatment within the office, a claim should be completed and the treatment information should be provided to the Human Resources Department with the claim.

Employees filing a claim for Carpal Tunnel must obtain approval before scheduling surgery. Failure to do so may result in disqualification of a Workers’ Compensation claim.

If/when an employee misses work to attend appointments for treatment of a work-related injury/illness, but is not disabled, the employee should be paid for the time lost.

Mileage to and from treatment is compensable only when the employee is treated outside the county of employment. Reimbursement for mileage is from the employee’s home to the treatment location and back. There is no reimbursement for trips to the pharmacy or other non-treatment appointments.

Medical bills or supporting documents should be sent to the Human Resources Department on previously submitted claims for processing/payment.

Workers’ Compensation will cover lost time in accordance with temporary total disability and temporary partial disability guidelines. Disability (lost time) indemnity benefits pick up when a person has lost time (any increment of a day or a whole day) for more than 7 days. This is defined as 7 consecutive calendar days or 7 non-consecutive days in any pattern from the same injury. Once that has been met, the wage loss benefits begin day 8. When a person misses more than 21 days, the initial 7 days of wage loss is paid as well. This applies to only those situations where a physician "disables" the worker by either (1) taking a person off work or (2) is imposing physical work restrictions or (3) is restricting the number of hours an employee can work.

The Human Resources Consultant must be informed of the employee’s status for the duration of the claim.

Time off under Workers’ Compensation may also count toward the twelve (12) weeks of leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act that may be granted per leave year.
